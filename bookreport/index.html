The book Who is Bill Gates?, By Patricia Brennan Demuth, shows that you can accomplish a lot with perserverance and hard work. Bill was very interested in learning as a kid. He read the entire World Book Encyclopedia when he was 7. He started in Lakeside, a private school, in 7th grade. It turned out to be a great choice, that would forever change his life. It was there that he had access to a Teletype computer. At the time most colleges didn't even have computers. This allowed him to dive deep into the world of computer programming. A lot like Bill Gates, I feel fortunate to have computers around me, and I am able to do computer programming.

Later, as Bill went to Harvard, he discovered the Altair 8800 on the cover of Popular Electronics, during January 1975 in his sophomore year. He, and his friend Paul Allen, who was also from Lakeside, decided to write a programming language for the new computer. Bill contacted the company that made the Altair, and sold their software to them. Microsoft was born.

Bill ended up making a lot of money, and decided to donate 95% of it. He started a foundation to help the world. His foundation focuses on health, education, and poverty.
